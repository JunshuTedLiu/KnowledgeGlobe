Scientia potentia est.

The world is changing with the evolution of knowledge. Scholars and researchers from all around the globe put their effort on making innovative changes and delivering impacts to this world. Moreover, these scholars and their research progress lead to the evolution of knowledge that becomes essential parts of world history. That is why people think that scholar could be one great perspective of looking at the change of history.

By building a data analysis and visualization tool, people can now take a macroscopical view of a topic and its popularity even in a specific range of observation attributes, such as time, location, and subcategories. Besides, by creating a corpus generator and an NLP analysis tool, users can take a microscopical observation from the content of the academic publications. These two powerful tools as a bundle are named as Knowledge Globe (abbreviated as “KnoGlo”) because they are powered by a database that contains scholar data from all around the world.

Knowledge Globe is a series of tools that can achieve the goal mentioned above. It contains two major parts - a statistical data visualization tool, and a corpus generator along with an NLP analysis tool.

The data visualization tool allows users to analyze and visualize statistical metadata of online publications from Springer Nature, a global scientific publisher of books and journals, in order to show the number of documents that were published under specific ranges of observations, which can be understood as filters, includes subjects, keyword tags, country, publisher, year, and publication type. By looking at those statistics, users can see the popularities of scientific topics in multiple observation perspective, like what gains more attention in a specific range of time or space.

The corpus generator and NLP tool allows users to create a corpus of the titles and abstracts of the populations with selected filters, and help them to take an in-depth look at the contents from their corpus by using NLP research techniques including Topic Modeling.

What KnoGlo can answer...

KnoGlo will give you a statistical result about a topic that you are interested in, within a range of place (country) and/or time (years).

For example,

  • "In which country, researchers show more interests and put efforts on the field of the Internet?"
  • "In the United States, when did Social Media start to be mentioned in publications?"
  • "How many articles about LGBTQ was published, compare with 1965 and 2015?"
  • "What kind of specific topics (subjects/keywords) do researchers focused on in the field of digital media in 2017?"

Where are the data come from?

Standing on the shoulder of giants.

Our tool is able to gather information from Springer Nature, which is one of the world’s leading scholar database.

Powered by Springer Nature metadata API that provides metadata for academic and professional publications, we could access 12 million online documents, including journal articles, book chapters, and protocols.

Using KnoGlo with the help of Springer Nature’s massive database, we could analyze the statistic information about how many articles published in selected topic and time ranges.
